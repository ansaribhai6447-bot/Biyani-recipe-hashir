Biryani Recipe - Layering & Dum

Biryani – Layering & Dum Process

Layering for Dum

Now comes the most important part — layering the biryani:

  1. Take a heavy-bottomed pot (so rice doesn’t burn).
  2. Spread a thin layer of cooked chicken with gravy at the bottom.
  3. Cover with a layer of parboiled rice (half of the rice).
  4. Sprinkle fried onions, chopped mint, chopped coriander, saffron milk, and a drizzle of ghee.
  5. Repeat layers if you have more chicken & rice.
  6. Finish with rice on top, and garnish with fried onions, mint, and a final spoon of ghee.

Dum Cooking (Steaming)

The “dum” is slow cooking with steam, which gives biryani its aroma and taste:

  • Seal the pot with a tight lid (or dough around the edges for airtight seal).
  • First, heat on high flame for 5 minutes to build steam.
  • Then lower to the smallest flame and cook for 25–30 minutes.
  • Alternatively, place the sealed pot in a preheated oven at 180°C (350°F) for 20–25 minutes.
  • Turn off the heat and let it rest (sealed) for 10–15 minutes before opening.

Result: Fluffy, aromatic rice with perfectly cooked chicken and layers infused with flavor.

Layering & Dum Tips

  • Always start with chicken at the bottom so rice absorbs flavor.
  • Don’t over-layer — 2–3 layers are enough.
  • Use a tava (flat pan) under the pot for even heat and to prevent burning.
  • Resting time is crucial: never open immediately after dum.

Quick Timing (Layering + Dum)

  • Layering: 10–15 minutes
  • Dum cooking: 25–35 minutes
  • Resting: 10 minutes
